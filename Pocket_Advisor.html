Pocket Advisor Case Study

Overview

Pocket Advisor is a money-saving app that helps people save money for a future expenditure or purchase. It guides users how to keep on track easily with their expenses, incomes and savings.

The Challenge

Create a responsive web app for people who do not like to deal with their finances. Give users clear information about what they can do to better save.

The Solution

Pocket Advisor gives clear and accessible information about the user's finances status. It is based on the 50-30-20 financial rule. It helps the user to keep on track of their expenses, incomes and savings.

The Design Process

To find a solution for the problem, I followed the steps of the User-Centered Design.

Discovery

Defining the Problem

People who don't like to deal with their finances assume that saving money is impossible. Saving money without a plan is really hard.

Competitor Analysis

To understand what other similar products are out there, I conducted an UX competitor analysis for responsive web apps intuit Mint, Finanzguru, Money Stats and Monefy.

Main insights:

Layout:

- The difference between pressed and unpressed button should be clear for the user.

- Highlightening the key words in the text makes it easier to understand what the most important information is.

Usability:

- The transition from one page to another should happen fast. The user should see the progress of the loading page.

- Having a good security for a financial web app gives user trust to use it.

Navigation:

- The navigation on the Home screen should be simple and accessible.

- Consistency in navigation on both the mobile versions as well as on desktop version is essential.

- Too many charts without explanations can overwhelm the user.

Compatibility:

- Optimizing the webpage with the keywords makes it easier to find in Google search.

- An app accesible on both Android and iOS gives it credibility and trust.

- Having a webpage for the mobile apps speaks about the product seriousness.

Call to action:

- Calls to action should differentiate from the other text.

- Clear and concise calls to action determine the user to follow them.

Differentiate:

- Calls to action that will motivate the user to take them.

- Shorter text with clear and concise information.

- Assurances about the security system of the web app.

Concepting

User Stories

1) As a new user, I need to be able to create a profile, so that the financial advice I receive is personalized to my me, my spending habits and tailored to my goals.

2) As a user, I want to see a dashboard of my finances clearly and visually, so that I can see how much I am spending and what at a glance.

3) As a user, I need to be able to tell the tool what my saving goal is and how long I have to reach it, so that I can save accordingly.

User Flow Diagram

Based on the competitor analysis and user stories, I created first the user flow diagrams for each of the functionalities.

At the end I gathered all the user flow diagrams in a single one. The main functionalities of the web app are:

- Create a personalized profile

- See the dashboard of the finances

- Create a saving goal.

Wireframing

After creating the user flow diagram, I started sketching the low-fidelity wireframes with the main functionalities.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Based on low-fidelity wireframes, I built the mid-fidelity wireframes.

Prototyping & User Testing

Prototyping & User Testing

After designing the mid-fidelity wireframes, I created a prototype in inVision. I conducted a scenario-based user testing with more than ten users. I tested the main functionalities of the app.

User Testing Analysis

I categorized the problems in critical and preferential.

Critical problems:

- Confuzing diagrams of expenses and incomes.

Recommendation

Chanange the correct numbers in the diagram, make a percentage difference through color highlights.

No exit option from the "Expenses" and "Incomes" screens.

Recommendation

Create a X button in the top right of the Expenses and Incomes screens/make them separate (not like windows on other screens)

No Overview for the total amount of money the user saved or spent in a month.

Recommendation

Consider writing an Overview for saved and spent money on the Home screen.

Preferential problems:

The user doesn't know exactly what the moeny are saved for.

Recommendation

Introduce an icon or an UI element that would show the user what his/her priority goals are.

Implementing the Feedback

I designed a separate screen for Expenses Overview. I changed the way of presenting the month on the screen.

I added details about the saved money and highlightened the saving priorities.

Final UI Design

Branding

Pocket Advisor's Mission

Help people save rapid and painless for their future dreams.

Guiding principles

Reliability. Pocket Advisor is a reliable tool. It protects the user's data and keeps its promises

Mutual trust. The users trust Pocket Advisor and Pocket Advisor trusts the users.

Simplicity. Pockets Advisor is a simple tool accessible for anyone.

Cooperation. Pocket Advisor doesn't function alone. The feedback of it's users is essential.

Typeface Choice

I chose Soleil typeface because it communicates freshness, dynamism and contemporary. It's a typeface based on the modernist ideas of simplicity, clarity and reduction to the essential forms. It gives the product a friendly and approachable feeling.

Soleil is combined with San Francisco typeface. San Francisco expresses seriosity and high quality. Together they communicate trust and stability.

Color Palette Choice

The primary colors are emerald and pacific blue. Emerald is a color of abundance, prosperity and growth. It transmits the energy of life, the peace of nature, renewal and stability.

Pacific blue communicates creativity and intelligence. It stands for loyality, strenghts, wisdom and trust.

The secondary colors are purple heart and black panther. Purple heart stands for support and close bonds.

Black panthere is linked to feelings of protection and saving.

Icons

The chosen icons are meant to make the navigation through the app easier. They communicate the user where he/she can find what they are looking for.

Final Screens

Securitized Sign-in and Sign-up

To sign-in or sign-up, the user is asked to identify himself with the face ID or digital fingerprint. It gives the user trust that his/her bank details are safe.

Home/Overview Screen

On the Home/Overview screen, the user can see the total and remaining amount of money from the beginning until the end of the month.

Incomes Details Screen

The Incomes screen offers details about the monthly income that entered the account/s.

Expenses Details Screen

The Expenses screen offers details about how much money was spent and on what.

Goal Setting Screen

On the Goal Setting screen, the user can see the total amount of saving and the remaining to save to reach his/her goal. He/she can always set new saving goals.

Learnings

#1

People need safety reassurance when it comes to their finances

People are skeptical when it comes to introducing somewhere their bank details. That's why a digital product in fintech should give assure the user that it is safe to use. One of the biggest learning was to not regret the time spent on concepting and creating a design that gives users trust.

#2

Users do not always imagine the product as we do

One of the most important lesson I learnt doing this project is that the designer is not his user. He may think a project in a way and change completly his/her way of thinking after looking the real user interacting with the product. While we create and work on design concepts, we should always let some room for a potential change of direction after user testings.

#3

Simplicity in design looks elegant and professional

After testing with users the first version of design screens, I realized that users prefer simple and functional design for a financial app. With a simple, clear and elegant design it's easy to understand what the main functions of the product are and what each function does.
